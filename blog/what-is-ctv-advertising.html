What is CTV Advertising? A Guide for Local Businesses

By CTV HOMES | 5 min read

For decades, television advertising was a luxury reserved for massive national brands with million-dollar budgets. Local businesses were forced to choose between highly saturated, expensive local broadcast spots or abandoning TV entirely.

Enter Connected TV (CTV) advertising. CTV has fundamentally changed how television is bought, sold, and measured, leveling the playing field for local businesses.

What Exactly is Connected TV?

Connected TV refers to any television set that is connected to the internet. This includes smart TVs and standard TVs equipped with devices like Roku, Apple TV, Amazon Fire Stick, or gaming consoles.

When viewers stream content through apps like Hulu, Peacock, ESPN+, or Tubi on these devices, the commercials they see are CTV ads.

Why Local Businesses Need CTV

The magic of CTV isn't just that it plays on a TV screen—it's how the ads are delivered. Unlike traditional TV, where an ad is broadcast to an entire designated market area (DMA) regardless of who is watching, CTV advertising is delivered via the internet. This means it borrows the best tools from digital marketing:

  • Unmatched Precision: You aren't buying a time slot on a specific channel; you are buying an audience. You can target viewers down to specific zip codes and layer on demographic data to ensure your ad only shows to ideal customers.
  • Measurable Performance: Gone are the days of guessing if a TV ad worked. CTV is a performance marketing channel. You can track precisely how many people saw your ad and subsequently visited your website.
  • Affordability: Because you are only paying for impressions delivered directly to your target audience, there is zero wasted spend.

The "Performance TV" Difference

At CTV HOMES, we view CTV not just as a branding exercise, but as Performance TV. This means leveraging the big screen to drive direct, measurable outcomes—from lead generation to foot traffic. By combining the emotional impact of a 30-second commercial with the precise targeting of digital search, local businesses can dominate their markets like never before.
