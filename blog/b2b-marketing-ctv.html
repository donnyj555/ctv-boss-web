B2B Marketing on CTV: Reaching Decision Makers at Home

By CTV HOMES | 6 min read

When most marketers think of television advertising, they picture consumer products: cars, fast food, insurance, and laundry detergent. For decades, B2B companies ignored TV entirely, assuming the massive waste associated with broadcast made it impossible to target a niche audience of decision-makers.

Connected TV (CTV) has fundamentally rewritten those rules. B2B marketers are now realizing that CEOs, procurement managers, and IT directors stream TV just like everyone else—and CTV provides the exact tools needed to reach them.

The Myth of "Working Hours Only"

The traditional B2B playbook relies heavily on LinkedIn ads and cold emails sent between 9 AM and 5 PM on weekdays. But decision-makers aren't robots who power down at 5 PM. They watch live sports, prestige dramas, and evening news.

Engaging a decision-maker while they are relaxed in their living room, away from the flurry of their email inbox, offers a unique opportunity to build brand affinity and tell a longer, more compelling story through a 30-second commercial.

Precision B2B Targeting on the Big Screen

How do you ensure your ad for enterprise SaaS software or commercial HVAC repair only runs for relevant business owners? CTV platforms leverage the same massive data partnerships as digital networks. You can target audiences based on:

  • Job Title and Seniority: Serve ads to households where a C-level executive or VP of IT resides.
  • Company Size and Industry: Narrow your targeting to specific industries like healthcare or manufacturing.
  • Account-Based Marketing (ABM): Upload a list of your target companies, and the platform will find the IP addresses associated with those offices and the home IP addresses of their employees, serving them ads wherever they stream.

The Power of B2B Retargeting

The B2B sales cycle is notoriously long. A prospect might visit your website, read a whitepaper, and then entirely forget about your brand. CTV retargeting allows you to follow up with a high-impact TV commercial.

Imagine a scenario where a procurement manager researches your commercial cleaning services online. That evening, while watching a basketball game on Hulu, they see your high-definition commercial detailing your reliability and scale. It instantly elevates your brand above competitors who rely strictly on follow-up emails.
