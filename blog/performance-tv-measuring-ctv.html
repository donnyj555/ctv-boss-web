Verified Visits: How to Measure TV Ad ROI

By CTV BOSS | 5 min read

There's an old saying in marketing: "Half the money I spend on advertising is wasted; the trouble is I don't know which half." For decades, television was the worst offender of this rule.

Businesses bought ads for "brand awareness," hoping that a spike in sales months later was correlated to the TV spend. Performance TV is eliminating this guesswork entirely.

The Cross-Device Graph

Connected TV runs on internet data. When your commercial plays on the living room TV via a Roku or Apple TV, the CTV platform logs the IP address of that household.

The magic happens via the cross-device graph. Advanced ad technology maps all the digital devices (smartphones, laptops, tablets) sharing that same home IP address. We know the TV and the smartphone belong to the same household.

What is a Verified Visit?

If someone watches your commercial to completion on their TV, and then 12 hours later uses their smartphone from that same household to visit your website or fill out a lead form, that is logged as a Verified Visit.

This attribution window allows us to measure real, tangible ROI. You no longer have to ask "how did you hear about us?" Our dashboard will show you exactly how many website visits and conversions were directly driven by your CTV campaigns.

Optimizing for Outcomes

Because we can measure the outcome, we can automate the optimization. Performance TV behaves like Google or Facebook Ads—if a specific audience segment or TV network is driving a lower Cost Per Visit, the system automatically routes more budget to that segment, constantly fine-tuning your ROI.
